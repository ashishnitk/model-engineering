Deep learning neural networks represent a powerful subset of machine learning, fundamentally differing from traditional machine learning algorithms primarily in their architecture and approach to feature engineering.

### Deep Learning Neural Networks vs. Traditional Machine Learning Algorithms

**Traditional Machine Learning (ML)** algorithms often require human experts to manually select and extract relevant features from raw data, a process known as feature engineering. These algorithms are typically well-suited for structured, labeled data and tasks where interpretability is crucial. Examples include linear regression, support vector machines (SVMs), decision trees, and random forests. They generally require less computational power and can perform well with smaller datasets.

**Deep Learning (DL)**, on the other hand, utilizes artificial neural networks with multiple "hidden" layers, allowing them to automatically learn and extract hierarchical features directly from raw, often unstructured, data with minimal human intervention. Each layer in a deep network learns increasingly abstract representations; for instance, in image recognition, early layers might detect edges, while deeper layers recognize shapes or entire objects. This "deep" architecture enables them to uncover subtle, complex patterns that would be difficult for humans to define explicitly. Deep learning excels in complex tasks involving unstructured data like images, audio, and text, where a high level of abstraction is needed.
